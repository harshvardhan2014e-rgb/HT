🇨🇦 Welcome to Burnaby

Burnaby, Canada Burnaby, Canada, is a vibrant city located in the Lower Mainland region of British Columbia. It is the third-largest city in British Columbia, with a population of approximately 249,125 as of the 2021 census. The city is known for its beautiful natural surroundings, diverse culture, and high-quality public transportation system. Burnaby is served by SkyTrain's Expo Line and Millennium Line, with Metrotown station being the busiest on weekends and the second-busiest on weekdays in regional Vancouver's urban transit system. The city is also home to major employers such as Simon Fraser University and the British Columbia Institute of Technology, contributing to its reputation as a hub for education and research.

📍Location

Burnaby is a city in British Columbia, Canada, located immediately east of Vancouver in the Metro Vancouver region. Burnaby is situated in the Lower Mainland region of British Columbia, at the geographic centre of the Burrard Peninsula. It is bordered by the City of Vancouver to the west, the District of North Vancouver across the Burrard Inlet to the north, Port Moody and Coquitlam to the east, New Westminster and Surrey across the Fraser River to the southeast, and Richmond on Lulu Island to the southwest . The city covers an area of approximately 96.6–98.6 square kilometres (37–38 mi²) and has an elevation ranging from sea level to 370 meters (1,214 feet) atop Burnaby Mountain

The city covers approximately 98.6 square kilometres and has a population of more than 270,000 people. Burnaby is about 15 kilometres from Downtown Vancouver and approximately 20 kilometres from Vancouver International Airport (YVR). Residents enjoy fast transportation through the SkyTrain, buses, highways, and cycling routes.

👨‍👩‍👧 Population

Burnaby is one of the largest and fastest-growing cities in the province of British Columbia. It has an estimated population of approximately 285,000 residents, making it the third-largest city in the province after Vancouver and Surrey. Over the past several decades, Burnaby has experienced continuous population growth due to immigration, economic development, and new residential construction. Many people choose to live in Burnaby because it offers excellent public transportation, quality schools, employment opportunities, and easy access to other cities within Metro Vancouver. The city's population has increased from just under 13,000 residents in 1921 to over 249,000 as of the 2021 census. This growth is attributed to its location in the heart of Metro Vancouver, its diverse cultural landscape, and its appeal as a place to live, work, and play. Burnaby's population is diverse, with a mix of families, young professionals, students, and seniors, and it continues to attract newcomers due to its modern housing, transit access, and educational institutions.

One of Burnaby's most notable characteristics is its cultural diversity. People from many different countries and backgrounds have made Burnaby their home, creating a welcoming and inclusive community. Residents come from countries such as India, China, the Philippines, South Korea, Iran, and many European nations. This diversity is reflected throughout the city in its restaurants, shopping centres, cultural festivals, places of worship, and community events. English is the primary language used in schools, businesses, and government services, although many other languages are commonly spoken in homes and neighbourhoods across the city.

Career Opportunities for BIW and CAD Design Engineers

Burnaby and the surrounding Metro Vancouver region offer career opportunities for experienced professionals in Body-in-White (BIW) design and Computer-Aided Design (CAD). Although British Columbia does not have large automobile manufacturing plants like Ontario, many engineering consulting firms, advanced manufacturing companies, electric vehicle businesses, and industrial equipment manufacturers employ mechanical design engineers. Professionals with experience in BIW design, sheet metal components, closures, underbody structures, and body structure development can also find opportunities in nearby cities such as Vancouver, Richmond, Surrey, and Coquitlam.

Engineers with expertise in CATIA, Siemens NX, SolidWorks, AutoCAD, GD&T, weld fixtures, product development, and design validation are in demand for senior engineering and leadership roles. Companies value professionals who can lead design teams, review engineering drawings, coordinate with manufacturing teams, and deliver projects that meet quality, safety, and production requirements.

Salary Expectations

Salaries for experienced Mechanical Design Engineers in Burnaby and the Metro Vancouver region are generally competitive. The exact salary depends on experience, qualifications, the industry, and the employer. Engineers with experience in BIW (Body-in-White) design, CAD modelling, project leadership, and automotive product development are often considered for senior-level positions.
